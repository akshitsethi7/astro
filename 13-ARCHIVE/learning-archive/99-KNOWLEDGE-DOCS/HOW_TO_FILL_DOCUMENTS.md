# How to Fill the Learning Documents

## The Right Approach

You now have:
- ✅ 60 structured document templates
- ✅ 45 PDF books with source material
- ✅ Extraction showing which topics are where

The best way forward is to **actively fill the documents yourself** as you study. This creates deeper learning than passive reading.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Foundation Documents (Week 1-2)

#### For 02-planets.md:

1. **Open the document** in your editor
2. **Open BPHS PDF** (pages with planet content)
3. **Read BPHS sections** on planets
4. **Fill in the document**:
   - Write the introduction in your own words
   - Summarize classical principles (don't copy verbatim)
   - Add your understanding of modern application
   - Create exercises based on your chart
   - Document your findings

**Example**:
```markdown
## Classical Principles

### Sun (Surya)

**From BPHS Chapter 2**:
The Sun represents the soul (atma), father, authority, and government. 
It is a natural malefic but gives vitality and leadership when well-placed.

*My understanding*: In my chart, Sun is in [sign] in [house], which means...

**Key Principles**:
- Exalted in Aries, debilitated in Libra
- Rules Leo
- Significator for father, authority, government
- Natural malefic but gives vitality
```

### For Each Document:

1. **Read relevant PDF sections** (use extraction stats to find pages)
2. **Take notes** in your own words
3. **Fill the document sections**:
   - Introduction: Your understanding of the topic
   - Classical Principles: Summarized teachings (with attribution)
   - Modern Application: How you apply it
   - Practice Exercises: Based on your chart
   - Personal Application: Your actual chart analysis

## Practical Workflow

### Daily (1-2 hours):

1. **Choose one document** (e.g., 02-planets.md)
2. **Read relevant PDF pages** (20-30 pages)
3. **Take notes** in your own words
4. **Fill document sections** with your understanding
5. **Apply to your chart** immediately
6. **Save and review**

### Weekly:

- Complete 2-3 documents
- Review what you've filled in
- Test your understanding with exercises
- Adjust and enhance
